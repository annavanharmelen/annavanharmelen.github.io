My work

My current work focusses on internal selective attention and working memory in humans, and how the two interact.

My Master's thesis investigated this same topic, by studying stimulus-driven selective attention within visual working memory, using a combination of eye-tracking and behavioural experiments.

My Bachelor's thesis focussed on a different combination of human cognitive systems, by studying multisensory evidence accumulation in human audiovisual decisions.

In the second year of my Bachelor's I also wrote a short review on the connections between entorhinal grid cells and surrounding neuronal populations.
